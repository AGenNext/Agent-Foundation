# Foundational Principles

Founder is real.
Founder is the foundation.
Foundation encodes the founder's core belief so the organization can scale without losing coherence.

## Core thesis

```text
Founder is real.
Belief is real.
Foundation is real.
Contracts are real.
Platform is real.
Outcomes must be real.
```

A foundation without a real founder becomes doctrine.
A founder with real belief becomes foundation.

## Principle 1: Reality before abstraction

The foundation starts from real experience, real conviction, real risk, and real responsibility.

```text
Do not begin with abstract systems.
Begin with real belief tested against reality.
```

## Principle 2: Founder as source of coherence

The founder creates the first coherent center of gravity.

The founder holds:

```text
belief
judgment
taste
direction
responsibility
risk
sacrifice
```

The foundation preserves that coherence so others can build without losing the original intent.

## Principle 3: Belief becomes contract

A belief becomes useful only when it is encoded into shared contracts.

```text
Belief
  -> Principle
  -> Contract
  -> Platform
  -> Product
  -> Outcome
```

Contracts are how belief becomes repeatable.

## Principle 4: No evidence, no claim

Every claim must be traceable to evidence.

```text
No evidence without source.
No decision without evidence.
No artifact without provenance.
No outcome without trace.
```

## Principle 5: Language before scale

A community cannot scale without shared language.

Agent-Foundation defines the shared vocabulary, contracts, and provenance model so downstream platforms and products do not invent conflicting meanings.

```text
Shared language creates shared work.
```

## Principle 6: Foundation defines, platform enforces

Agent-Foundation defines base contracts and principles.

The platform enforces them at runtime.

```text
Foundation defines.
Platform enforces.
Products deliver.
Community contributes.
Partners amplify.
```

## Principle 7: Coherence before contribution

Open contribution works only when the center is clear.

```text
Community contributes.
Maintainers curate.
Founder-originated coherence remains protected.
```

Contribution should extend the foundation, not fragment it.

## Principle 8: Interoperability over lock-in

Foundation contracts must remain language-neutral, database-neutral, runtime-neutral, and vendor-neutral.

```text
No database lock-in.
No runtime lock-in.
No vendor lock-in.
No platform lock-in at the contract layer.
```

## Principle 9: Provenance by construction

Every source, evidence record, decision, artifact, theory, publication, agent, and runtime must be able to carry provenance.

Provenance is not metadata after the fact.
It is part of the foundation.

## Principle 10: Evaluation before trust

Trust must be earned through evaluation.

Agent-Foundation supports evaluation so agents, artifacts, theories, and outputs can be tested, compared, improved, and trusted.

```text
Trust follows evidence.
Evidence follows evaluation.
Evaluation follows clear contracts.
```

## Principle 11: Products deliver belief into reality

Products are not random features.
They are expressions of the foundation in real user workflows.

```text
Foundation preserves belief.
Platform operationalizes belief.
Product delivers belief.
Customer outcome proves belief.
```

## Principle 12: The organization is the autonomy boundary

Autonomy does not belong to rogue agents or isolated tools.

Autonomy belongs to the organization and is expressed through governed platforms, contracts, policies, and accountable participants.

## Principle 13: Community extends coherence

The community is not outside the foundation.
The community extends the foundation by improving contracts, examples, documentation, SDKs, integrations, and implementations.

The community does not erase origin.
It carries it forward.

## Principle 14: Partners amplify the foundation

Partners and providers do not replace the foundation.
They amplify it through scoped capabilities, commitments, evidence, and delivery.

## Principle 15: Outcomes close the loop

The foundation is only meaningful if it leads to real outcomes.

```text
Founder
  -> Belief
  -> Foundation
  -> Contracts
  -> Platform
  -> Products
  -> Community
  -> Outcomes
```

The loop closes when real users receive real value.

## Canonical statement

```text
Founder is the foundation.
The foundation encodes belief into contracts.
The platform enforces contracts.
Products deliver outcomes.
Community extends coherence.
Partners amplify capability.
Evidence proves value.
```

## One-line definition

The foundational principles of Agent-Foundation state that a real founder's core belief becomes a shared, evidence-backed, language-neutral contract layer that lets platforms, products, communities, and partners create real outcomes without losing coherence.
