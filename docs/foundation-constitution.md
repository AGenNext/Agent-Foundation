# Foundation Constitution

The Foundation Constitution defines what must not be violated as the AGenNext ecosystem grows.

A theory explains why.
A constitution defines the non-negotiables.

## Preamble

Founder is real.
Belief is real.
Foundation is real.
Contracts are real.
Outcomes must be real.

Agent-Foundation exists to preserve coherence while allowing platforms, products, communities, and partners to grow from the same origin.

## Article 1: Founder as Foundation

```text
Founder is the foundation.
```

The founder is the first source of coherence, belief, judgment, direction, and responsibility.

The foundation encodes that coherence so it can scale beyond the founder without becoming fragmented.

## Article 2: Reality Before Abstraction

No principle, contract, product, or platform should drift away from reality.

Every abstraction must remain testable against real use, real evidence, real outcomes, and real responsibility.

## Article 3: Evidence Before Claim

```text
No evidence, no claim.
No source, no evidence.
No provenance, no artifact.
No trace, no outcome.
```

Every important output must be traceable.

## Article 4: Contracts Before Runtime

The foundation defines language-neutral contracts before runtime implementation.

Runtime systems must implement contracts without distorting their meaning.

## Article 5: Coherence Before Scale

Growth must not break coherence.

New products, partners, providers, community contributions, and runtime implementations must align with the foundation language and principles.

## Article 6: Foundation Defines, Platform Enforces

```text
Foundation defines.
Platform enforces.
Products deliver.
Community contributes.
Partners amplify.
```

Agent-Foundation does not become the runtime.
The platform enforces the contracts defined by the foundation.

## Article 7: Interoperability Over Lock-In

Foundation contracts must remain:

```text
language-neutral
database-neutral
runtime-neutral
vendor-neutral
platform-neutral at the contract layer
```

## Article 8: Versioned Evolution

Breaking changes must not be hidden inside existing contracts.

Stable versions remain stable.
New incompatible semantics require new versions.

## Article 9: Community Extends Coherence

Community contribution is welcome when it extends the foundation without fragmenting it.

Maintainers curate coherence.
Contributors improve clarity, examples, contracts, tests, and implementation support.

## Article 10: Partners Amplify, They Do Not Replace

Partners and providers amplify the foundation through scoped commitments and capabilities.

They do not replace founder-originated coherence, foundation contracts, or platform accountability.

## Article 11: Evaluation Before Trust

Trust follows evidence.
Evidence follows evaluation.
Evaluation follows clear contracts.

No agent, artifact, theory, decision, publication, or runtime should be treated as trustworthy without evaluation appropriate to its risk.

## Article 12: Outcomes Close the Loop

The foundation is meaningful only when it leads to real outcomes.

```text
Reality
  -> Belief
  -> Principles
  -> Contracts
  -> Platform
  -> Products
  -> Outcomes
  -> Reality
```

Outcomes must return evidence to the foundation and improve the system.

## Non-Negotiables

```text
Do not erase origin.
Do not fragment vocabulary.
Do not break contract compatibility silently.
Do not assert without evidence.
Do not hide provenance.
Do not turn the foundation into runtime enforcement.
Do not let products redefine base contracts locally.
Do not let contribution become incoherence.
Do not let partners replace platform accountability.
Do not let outcomes become imaginary.
```

## One-line constitution

The Foundation Constitution protects the founder-originated coherence of AGenNext by requiring reality, evidence, provenance, language-neutral contracts, versioned evolution, community contribution, partner amplification, and outcome validation.
