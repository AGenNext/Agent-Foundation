# Manifesto

Agents are becoming part of how people learn, work, decide, build, govern, and collaborate.

This change should not be understood only by vendors, experts, or large organizations. Communities need open education, shared language, responsible awareness, and interoperable foundations so they can participate safely and meaningfully.

Agent-Foundation exists for that purpose.

It is an open community education and awareness initiative for the agent ecosystem. It is non-profit, non-commercial, and focused on community welfare.

## We believe agent education should be open

People should be able to learn what agents are, how they work, what they can do, where they fail, and how they should be governed.

Agent education should not be locked behind proprietary language, vendor marketing, or inaccessible technical complexity.

## We believe awareness must come before adoption

Communities should not be pushed into agentic systems before they understand the risks, responsibilities, limitations, and opportunities.

Responsible adoption starts with awareness.

## We believe shared language reduces harm

Confusion creates risk.

The agent ecosystem needs shared terms for agents, tools, memory, evidence, decisions, evaluation, governance, provenance, runtime, publication, and interoperability.

Agent-Foundation exists to help define and maintain that shared language.

## We believe evidence matters

Claims about agent systems should be traceable.

Evaluations should explain what was tested. Decisions should explain what evidence was used. Publications should show where information came from.

No evidence, no trust.

## We believe interoperability protects communities

Communities should not be forced into one vendor, one model, one framework, one database, one cloud, or one runtime.

Open contracts and standards-aligned definitions help people move, compare, evaluate, and govern agent systems across environments.

## We believe foundations must remain separate from products

A foundation should define shared public-good knowledge, terminology, and contracts.

Products may implement those foundations, but the foundation itself should not become a commercial delivery vehicle.

This separation keeps Agent-Foundation useful to the wider ecosystem.

## We believe community welfare is the north star

The purpose of Agent-Foundation is not hype, ownership, or control.

The purpose is to help communities understand, evaluate, and responsibly adopt agentic systems through open education, awareness, research, standards, and interoperability.

## Our commitment

Agent-Foundation will work to publish and maintain:

- Open agent education material.
- Public awareness resources.
- Shared ecosystem vocabulary.
- Language-neutral contracts.
- Research-grounded models.
- Standards alignment notes.
- Provenance and traceability guidance.
- Evaluation and benchmarking literacy.
- Responsible adoption guidance.

## Closing statement

The future of agents should be understandable, interoperable, traceable, and responsible.

Agent-Foundation exists to make that future open to the community.
