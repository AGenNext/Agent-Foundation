# Principles

Agent-Foundation is guided by principles that protect its role as an open community education and awareness initiative for the agent ecosystem.

These principles help keep the foundation useful, trustworthy, non-profit, non-commercial, and aligned with community welfare.

## 1. Community welfare first

Foundation work should improve public understanding, responsible adoption, and shared access to agent knowledge.

The foundation exists to serve communities, learners, researchers, builders, institutions, and organizations, not to advance a single product or vendor agenda.

## 2. Education before adoption

People should be able to understand agentic systems before they are expected to use, govern, buy, or deploy them.

Agent-Foundation should explain concepts clearly, reduce confusion, and make agent education accessible to both technical and non-technical audiences.

## 3. Evidence before claims

Important claims should be supported by evidence, sources, rationale, or clearly stated assumptions.

No strategic decision, evaluation, benchmark, or publication should be treated as reliable without traceable evidence.

## 4. Provenance by design

Sources, evidence, decisions, evaluations, artifacts, theories, and publications should be traceable.

The foundation should make it possible to understand where information came from, how it was used, and what it influenced.

## 5. Open standards before proprietary definitions

Agent-Foundation should align with open standards wherever possible.

New terminology or contracts should only be introduced when existing standards are insufficient, and the reason should be documented.

## 6. Interoperability over lock-in

Foundation contracts should work across languages, tools, agents, runtimes, and databases.

The foundation should not depend on a single vendor, model provider, framework, database, cloud, or runtime.

## 7. Stability over novelty

Shared contracts should be stable enough for other repositories and communities to depend on.

Breaking changes must move to a new version package. Novel ideas should mature through documentation, discussion, and review before becoming canonical contracts.

## 8. Public good before commercial advantage

Agent-Foundation exists for public-good education, awareness, research, standards, and interoperability.

Commercial product delivery, monetization, customer-specific implementation, and proprietary runtime behavior belong outside this repository.

## 9. Transparency over opacity

Foundation work should be explainable.

Definitions, decisions, trade-offs, limitations, and assumptions should be documented in language that communities can understand and reuse.

## 10. Responsible adoption over hype

Agentic systems should be understood, evaluated, governed, and adopted responsibly.

The foundation should avoid hype-driven language and instead support practical education, awareness, risk understanding, evaluation literacy, and community welfare.

## 11. Shared language over fragmentation

The ecosystem needs common terms for agents, tools, memory, evidence, decisions, evaluations, governance, runtime, and publication.

Agent-Foundation should reduce fragmentation by defining shared vocabulary that other repositories can reuse.

## 12. Foundation before implementation

Agent-Foundation defines shared concepts and contracts.

Implementation repositories build products, runtimes, user experiences, integrations, and deployment systems from those foundations.

This separation keeps the foundation open, reusable, and useful beyond any single product.
