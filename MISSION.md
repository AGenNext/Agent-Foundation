# Mission

Agent-Foundation is an open community education and awareness initiative for the agent ecosystem.

It exists as the canonical non-profit and non-commercial foundation initiative of AGenNext, focused on community welfare through education, awareness, research, open standards, and shared ecosystem contracts.

## Mission statement

To make agent education, awareness, research, standards, and interoperability openly accessible for the benefit of communities, learners, researchers, builders, institutions, and organizations.

## Why this exists

Agentic systems are becoming part of how people learn, work, build, decide, govern, and collaborate. Communities need open knowledge, shared vocabulary, responsible guidance, and interoperable foundations so they can understand and adopt these systems safely.

Agent-Foundation exists to reduce confusion, fragmentation, and unequal access by publishing open educational material, shared terminology, research-grounded models, and portable contracts for the agent ecosystem.

## Community welfare focus

Community welfare means helping people and organizations understand agentic systems in ways that are:

- Accessible to learners and non-experts.
- Useful for builders and researchers.
- Responsible for institutions and communities.
- Transparent about evidence, assumptions, and limitations.
- Aligned with open standards and public-good outcomes.

## What we support

Agent-Foundation supports:

- Agent education.
- Public awareness.
- Research literacy.
- Open standards.
- Shared terminology.
- Interoperable contracts.
- Evidence-based decision models.
- Evaluation and benchmarking literacy.
- Provenance and traceability.
- Responsible adoption guidance.

## What we avoid

Agent-Foundation avoids:

- Commercial product ownership.
- Customer-specific implementation.
- Proprietary runtime logic.
- Billing and monetization workflows.
- Closed or vendor-locked definitions.
- Untraceable claims presented as fact.

## Relationship to AGenNext products

Agent-Foundation defines public-good knowledge, terminology, and contracts.

AGenNext product and implementation repositories may consume these contracts, but commercial product delivery belongs outside Agent-Foundation.

This separation keeps the foundation open, educational, standards-aligned, and useful to the wider community while allowing implementation repositories to build production systems responsibly.
